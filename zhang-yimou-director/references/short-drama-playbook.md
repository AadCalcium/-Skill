# 短剧转译手册

Use this file when generating or rewriting short-drama scripts, especially vertical 9:16 episodes for Douyin/Kuaishou/短剧平台. The goal is to compress Zhang-inspired cinematic thinking into fast, readable, producible short drama.

## Core Formula

```text
张艺谋启发短剧 = 社会压力 + 仪式规则 + 视觉母题 + 小人物选择 + 图像反转
```

Every episode should answer:

- Who is being watched?
- What rule are they forced to obey?
- What object proves the rule is unfair?
- What small action breaks the pattern?
- What image makes the audience want the next episode?

## 3-Second Hook Types

### 1. Ritual Violation Hook

Open on someone breaking a visible rule.

Examples:

- Bride throws the toast wine onto the family ledger.
- Employee wipes their name off the company honor wall.
- Daughter-in-law turns off every red lamp before dinner.
- Teacher locks the school gate with all parents watching.

Use when:

- The story involves family hierarchy, workplace rules, village customs, or public shame.

### 2. Object Evidence Hook

Open on a close-up object that changes the case.

Examples:

- A red envelope falls open with a hospital bill inside.
- A schoolbag drips water in the principal's office.
- A phone plays one second of deleted footage.
- A wedding shoe has mud from a forbidden place.

Use when:

- The story is suspense, revenge, justice, or hidden truth.

### 3. Crowd Judgment Hook

Open with the protagonist surrounded.

Examples:

- Four elders sit in a row; one empty chair is left for the accused.
- Neighbors hold phones in a circle around a woman at the stairwell.
- Coworkers stand behind glass while one person signs resignation.
- A village loudspeaker calls one name again and again.

Use when:

- The story needs public pressure fast.

### 4. Color Shock Hook

Open with one color invading a neutral world.

Examples:

- A white wedding dress is stamped with a red company seal.
- Black umbrellas fill a sunlit schoolyard.
- Gold confetti falls while someone is being fired.
- A red scarf is tied over a surveillance camera.

Use when:

- The script has weak visual identity.

### 5. Sound-Cut Hook

Open with repeated sound, then sudden silence.

Examples:

- Stamp, stamp, stamp. Silence. The clerk refuses the last paper.
- Wedding drums stop when the bride says "少了一个人."
- Chopsticks click in a banquet hall, then one bowl cracks.
- A phone vibrates inside an urn box.

Use when:

- The scene is dialogue-heavy and needs sensory force.

## Episode Structures

### 60-Second Single Episode

```text
0-3s: image hook
3-10s: rule becomes clear
10-25s: protagonist is pressured to obey
25-40s: object or witness disrupts the rule
40-53s: public reversal
53-60s: image cliffhanger
```

### 90-Second Dramatic Episode

```text
0-5s: ritual violation or object evidence
5-20s: establish social order
20-40s: first accusation
40-60s: protagonist's hidden strategy
60-78s: crowd turns or authority intervenes
78-90s: visual cliffhanger
```

### 3-Episode Mini Arc

Episode 1: The ritual traps the hero.

- Hook: public humiliation.
- End: object appears but meaning is unknown.

Episode 2: The hero learns the system.

- Hook: hidden rule is revealed.
- End: hero uses the ritual against the controller.

Episode 3: The public verdict reverses.

- Hook: everyone gathers.
- End: first image returns with opposite meaning.

## Low-Budget Spectacle

Use pattern, not scale:

- 4 people seated symmetrically can feel like a court.
- 8 coworkers in identical lanyards can feel like an institution.
- 12 red bowls on a table can feel like ancestral pressure.
- Offscreen crowd sound can make a room feel watched.
- One LED sign/reflection can make a city feel oppressive.
- One corridor repeated from different angles can feel like a maze.

Budget swaps:

| Expensive | Short-drama replacement |
| --- | --- |
| Palace courtyard | hotel banquet room, ancestral hall, old apartment corridor |
| Mass crowd | phone screens, offscreen murmurs, tight ring of relatives |
| Battlefield | snow-covered rooftop, parking garage, abandoned factory |
| Imperial costume | formal suits, uniforms, wedding outfits, staff aprons |
| Giant ceremony | family dinner seating rule, company award ceremony, school assembly |

## Character Engines

### The Stubborn Seeker

Inspired by realist persistence.

- Wants one sentence: apology, explanation, name, receipt, signature.
- Everyone says "算了."
- She/he repeats the demand until the system exposes itself.
- Best ending: the system gives justice in a morally painful form.

Use for:

- consumer rights, rural conflict, school bullying, workplace injustice.

### The Silent Strategist

Inspired by trapped-woman dramas.

- Appears obedient.
- Learns the ritual better than its owner.
- Moves objects quietly.
- Breaks the system at the public moment.

Use for:

- marriage, family property, domestic oppression, beauty salon/workshop stories.

### The Accidental Witness

Inspired by memory/evidence films.

- Does not want to be heroic.
- Holds one image, recording, bill, or memory fragment.
- Pressure forces them to decide whether to reveal it.

Use for:

- suspense, legal, urban corruption, secret parentage.

### The Comic Functionary

Inspired by type-film pressure cooker.

- Works inside the system but sees its absurdity.
- Uses jokes, delays, procedures, and misdirection.
- Final action becomes morally serious.

Use for:

- legal comedy, workplace, community office, police/court-adjacent stories.

## Scene Recipes

### Family Banquet Verdict

Ingredients:

- Table order, repeated toast, one forbidden seat, one object under a bowl.
- Color: gold/red becoming dirty or oppressive.
- Sound: chopsticks, glass clink, forced laughter.

Beat:

1. Everyone waits for the protagonist to toast.
2. Elder announces a rule.
3. Protagonist notices the object.
4. Protagonist changes seating or refuses the cup.
5. Silence exposes the real hierarchy.

### Office Ceremony Collapse

Ingredients:

- KPI wall, lanyards, uniform applause, projector, resignation form.
- Color: cold white/blue with one red seal.
- Sound: microphone feedback, stamp, applause loop.

Beat:

1. Boss awards "family culture" certificate.
2. One employee is asked to apologize publicly.
3. The certificate hides evidence.
4. Employee uses the microphone to replay one recording.
5. Applause becomes silence.

### Village Mediation

Ingredients:

- Long table, tea cups, red stamp, loudspeaker, plastic stools.
- Color: earth/yellow, one red document.
- Sound: loudspeaker, dogs, distant tractor, stamp.

Beat:

1. Cadre says "都是一家人."
2. Victim asks for one apology.
3. Villagers pressure them to settle.
4. Hidden injury/report appears.
5. Settlement turns into official case.

### Schoolyard Reckoning

Ingredients:

- Roll call, red scarf, empty desk, schoolbag, bell.
- Color: white/blue order with red scarf.
- Sound: bell, children reciting, chalk.

Beat:

1. Teacher counts students.
2. One name receives no answer.
3. Authority wants to erase the absence.
4. The schoolbag returns wet/torn.
5. Children repeat the missing name together.

### Wedding As Trap

Ingredients:

- Door games, red envelopes, veil, family camera, shoe.
- Color: red joy turning blood/shame.
- Sound: suona/drums, camera shutter, crowd laugh.

Beat:

1. Groom's family demands one last ritual.
2. Bride notices object/evidence.
3. Everyone chants for compliance.
4. Bride performs the ritual incorrectly on purpose.
5. The wedding video becomes evidence.

### Hospital Window

Ingredients:

- Payment window, queue, number ticket, medical bill, curtain.
- Color: white/green with red stamp/blood.
- Sound: queue number, printer, cough, fluorescent hum.

Beat:

1. Protagonist reaches window after long wait.
2. One missing stamp blocks treatment.
3. Crowd asks them to leave the line.
4. They place the patient/object at the window.
5. The rule meets a human body.

### Urban Corruption Room

Ingredients:

- Glass wall, black car key, LED city reflection, contract, surveillance feed.
- Color: neon blue/black/gold.
- Sound: elevator ding, phone vibration, distant traffic.

Beat:

1. Powerful figure smiles behind glass.
2. Contract is offered as mercy.
3. Reflection reveals who else is watching.
4. The protagonist signs the wrong name deliberately.
5. The glass room becomes a witness box.

## Dialogue Compression Rules

Make every line playable and subtitle-friendly:

- One line should carry one pressure.
- Let elders speak in rules, not emotions.
- Let young characters speak in facts or refusals.
- Let villains sound reasonable.
- Let comedy come from procedure, not random jokes.

Rewrite pattern:

Weak:

```text
你们一直欺负我，我今天一定要揭露你们所有人的真面目。
```

Stronger:

```text
这杯酒，我不敬。
```

Weak:

```text
我这么多年受了很多委屈，你们根本不知道我的痛苦。
```

Stronger:

```text
这把钥匙，我拿了七年。今天还你。
```

## Image Cliffhangers

Use one of these endings:

- Object changes owner.
- Ritual stops midway.
- Crowd turns toward a new person.
- A sealed door opens.
- A color contaminates the clean frame.
- A recording plays but cuts before the key line.
- A document is stamped with the wrong name.
- Someone sits in a forbidden chair.
- The protagonist repeats the opening gesture with opposite meaning.

## Production Checklist

Before delivering a short-drama script:

- Is the first frame a conflict image?
- Can the episode be shot in 1-3 locations?
- Does the hero make one clear choice?
- Is there a recurring object?
- Does public pressure appear visually?
- Are lines short enough for subtitles?
- Is the cliffhanger an image?
- Is the visual style original rather than scene-copying?
