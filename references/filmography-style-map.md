# 张艺谋影片风格地图

Use this file when the user asks for a fuller Zhang Yimou style, mentions a specific film, or needs to choose which period of Zhang-inspired language fits a short-drama project. Verify exact factual claims when answering public-history questions; this file is a craft map, not a current filmography database.

## How To Use

1. Identify the user's story genre and production budget.
2. Choose one primary film-period reference and one secondary reference.
3. Extract the transferable craft principle: color logic, space, ritual, object, crowd, sound, reversal.
4. Recombine into an original short-drama scene. Do not copy exact scenes, character names, dialogue, or shot sequences.

## Period 0: Cinematographer Roots

### 《一个和八个》《黄土地》《老井》

Core lessons:

- Image before plot: the frame carries historical and emotional weight.
- Land is not background; it is a moral and social force.
- High horizon, empty space, isolated human figures, and rough texture make individuals feel small inside history.
- Composition can be deliberately off-center to make the world feel unsettled.

Short-drama translation:

- Use one location as destiny: a factory gate, old village road, hospital corridor, schoolyard, banquet entrance.
- Put the protagonist at the edge of frame before they gain agency.
- Use silence and environmental sound before dialogue.
- Let the first wide shot state the social condition.

Best for:

- Rural justice, family history, poverty, inheritance, migration, childhood, old debts.

Avoid:

- Empty "黄土地" styling when the story is urban or has no land/fate pressure.

## Period 1: Red Folk Allegory

### 《红高粱》

Craft DNA:

- Red as vitality, desire, blood, risk, collective life.
- Folk song, wine, dust, sun, bodies, and rough gestures create a physical world.
- A woman's desire can become the engine of community, not merely romance.
- The story can feel mythic while still being earthy and tactile.

Short-drama translation:

- Use one sensual material: sorghum, cloth, chili oil, wedding wine, red paper, soil, steam, sweat.
- Make rebellion bodily: standing up, refusing a cup, spilling wine, lifting a veil, breaking a queue.
- Build a public ritual, then let one private desire disrupt it.

Scene recipe:

- At a village wedding banquet, everyone waits for the bride to toast the creditor. She raises the red cup, then pours it onto the ledger instead.

Avoid:

- Treating "red" as decoration. Red must mean life force or danger.

### 《菊豆》

Craft DNA:

- Enclosed space as sexual and social prison.
- Dye vats, cloth, and saturated color make desire visible.
- The family system turns bodies into property.
- Looking, overhearing, and hidden passageways create moral claustrophobia.

Short-drama translation:

- Use workplace or family business as prison: textile shop, photo studio, clinic, beauty salon, noodle shop, repair store.
- Make color a byproduct of labor: dye, flour, blood, paint, makeup, ink.
- Build a forbidden glance system: through curtains, shelves, monitors, windows.

Avoid:

- Turning oppression into exotic eroticism. The trapped character needs strategy and choice.

### 《大红灯笼高高挂》

Craft DNA:

- Ritual turns desire into ranking.
- Courtyard geometry, doors, corridors, foot massage, lantern lighting, and meal order are power mechanisms.
- Repetition is terrifying when every repeat decides status.
- The household is a machine.

Short-drama translation:

- Replace concubine courtyard with modern ranking systems: family group chat, company KPI board, live-stream ranking, inheritance dinner, school award wall.
- Invent one ritual that determines who receives resources.
- Let the protagonist break the ritual with a small wrong action.

Scene recipe:

- In a family-owned hotel, each daughter-in-law's room light decides who gets the old lady's medical card. The new wife unscrews every bulb before the dinner bell.

Avoid:

- Lantern cosplay. Focus on the rule, ranking, and humiliation mechanism.

## Period 2: Realist Moral Persistence

### 《秋菊打官司》

Craft DNA:

- One stubborn ordinary person keeps asking for an explanation.
- Bureaucracy is a maze of rooms, windows, receipts, waiting, stamps, and polite refusal.
- The moral force comes from procedural repetition.
- The ending can be ethically complicated: justice arrives in a form the seeker did not want.

Short-drama translation:

- Give the hero a tiny but non-negotiable demand: apology, signature, refund, name correction, public clarification.
- Escalate through institutions: village committee -> police station -> hospital -> court -> media.
- Use one repeated line: "我要个说法."
- Let the final result hurt someone the hero did not intend to destroy.

Best for:

- Local disputes, workplace injustice, family insult, consumer rights, medical conflict.

### 《活着》

Craft DNA:

- Individual lives are bent by history, but the tone remains human, domestic, and sometimes absurd.
- Objects and folk performance carry memory.
- Tragedy is stronger when surrounded by ordinary meals, jokes, children, and small survival tasks.

Short-drama translation:

- Use a family object across years: puppet box, sewing machine, enamel bowl, school certificate, cassette tape.
- Let each episode jump to a new consequence while the object survives.
- Do not write history lectures; show how policy/social change enters kitchen, hospital, school, factory.

Avoid:

- Pure misery stacking. The characters must keep living.

### 《一个都不能少》

Craft DNA:

- Minimal objective becomes moral epic.
- Non-professional texture and real locations create urgency.
- The protagonist is inexperienced but relentless.
- A public broadcast can turn private search into collective witness.

Short-drama translation:

- A substitute teacher, temp worker, junior employee, or elder caretaker refuses to lose "one person."
- Use counting as suspense: one less student, one missing package, one absent worker, one unpaid name.
- Make the city a hostile system of signs, fares, windows, and indifference.

### 《我的父亲母亲》

Craft DNA:

- Memory softens color; love is built through repeated routes, waiting, and handmade objects.
- A path can become emotional architecture.
- Simple actions gain force through repetition.

Short-drama translation:

- Use one repeated road: bus stop, alley, stairs, corridor, bridge.
- Let an object of care recur: lunchbox, scarf, repaired shoe, hand-copied note.
- Build romance through action, not declarations.

## Period 3: Color-Coded Spectacle And Power

### 《英雄》

Craft DNA:

- Multiple versions of truth can be color-coded.
- Minimal dialogue, maximal formal control.
- Power is expressed through scale, symmetry, distance, and disciplined bodies.
- The story asks whether personal revenge, love, and political order can coexist.

Short-drama translation:

- Use color-coded testimony: each character's version of an event has a different palette and object arrangement.
- For budget, create three retellings in the same room by changing lighting, props, and costume accents.
- Let the final truth be visually plainer than the lies.

Avoid:

- Copying wuxia duel imagery. Extract "conflicting versions of truth".

### 《十面埋伏》

Craft DNA:

- Beauty can be camouflage for ambush.
- Movement through nature tracks desire and betrayal.
- Sound and rhythm can locate hidden threat.

Short-drama translation:

- In a modern thriller, use a beautiful place as trap: tea house, photo studio, wedding lawn, bamboo-themed restaurant, luxury homestay.
- Make sound the clue: bracelet, tap code, elevator ding, chopsticks, drum beat, notification tone.

### 《满城尽带黄金甲》

Craft DNA:

- Gold and symmetry express palace-level rot.
- Family conflict is staged as state ceremony.
- Costume, medicine, architecture, and meal ritual carry betrayal.
- Excess can become suffocation.

Short-drama translation:

- Use a rich family banquet, corporate annual meeting, hotel wedding, or clan ancestral hall.
- Make luxury oppressive: gold decor, identical uniforms, perfect seating, forced smiles.
- The truth should break the table order.

## Period 4: Memory, War, And Moral Shelter

### 《金陵十三钗》

Craft DNA:

- Sanctuary under siege.
- Performance, costume, and mistaken identity become survival tactics.
- Moral choice occurs when ordinary people must protect others at cost.

Short-drama translation:

- Use "shelter" settings: school, clinic, basement, old theatre, women's dorm, small shop in a crisis.
- The protagonist begins as self-interested, then accepts witness/protector role.

### 《归来》

Craft DNA:

- Memory loss makes love procedural: the same waiting repeated.
- Political trauma is shown through domestic absence.
- Restraint is stronger than confrontation.

Short-drama translation:

- Use repeated appointment, station, doorway, old photo, voice recording.
- The climax can be someone choosing to keep showing up even when not recognized.

### 《一秒钟》

Craft DNA:

- Film itself is memory, desire, and evidence.
- The smallest fragment of image can be worth a journey.
- Dust, projection, screen light, and crowd watching create communal longing.

Short-drama translation:

- Use phone video, surveillance clip, lost livestream, old tape, deleted message.
- The hero needs "one second" of visual proof.

## Period 5: Ink, Doubles, And Tactical Minimalism

### 《影》

Craft DNA:

- Black-white-gray palette.
- Doubles, masks, substitute bodies, and tactical deception.
- Soft vs hard: umbrella vs blade, water vs metal, body vs strategy.
- The image is restrained, but the politics is sharp.

Short-drama translation:

- Use one monochrome location: rainy office, underground garage, funeral home, hospital, legal archive.
- Design a double: substitute bride, fake heir, stand-in employee, scapegoat, impersonated account.
- Use one soft object against a hard system: umbrella, paper, cloth, hairpin, medical gauze.

Avoid:

- Making everything gray without a tactical reason.

## Period 6: Type-Film Pressure Cooker

### 《悬崖之上》《狙击手》

Craft DNA:

- Snow, cold, secrecy, and surveillance.
- Team members act under incomplete information.
- Identity, sacrifice, and coded communication drive suspense.

Short-drama translation:

- Use a cold palette for moral tests: winter street, late-night office, hospital corridor, police station, data room.
- Make every character's phone, badge, or passcard a possible trap.

### 《满江红》

Craft DNA:

- Sealed space, ticking clock, ensemble deception, dark comedy, interrogation rhythm.
- Each reveal changes the function of earlier jokes or lies.
- The final emotion is collective and moral, not just clever.

Short-drama translation:

- Set one episode in one courtyard/office/restaurant back room during a deadline.
- Give every character a false job and a hidden allegiance.
- Use repeated running through corridors/stairs/elevators as comic pressure.
- End with a moral text/object being publicly spoken or exposed.

### 《坚如磐石》

Craft DNA:

- Urban neon, corruption, surveillance, violence beneath order.
- Institutional and family power overlap.
- Modern city can feel like a machine.

Short-drama translation:

- Use glass offices, parking garages, banquet rooms, bridge roads, phone screens, monitoring rooms.
- Make money/power visible through surfaces: reflections, LED, black cars, polished tables.

### 《第二十条》

Craft DNA:

- Legal/ethical debate becomes popular drama.
- Comedy and family life keep the institutional argument human.
- The climax reframes a rule around ordinary people's dignity.

Short-drama translation:

- Use one legal, school, hospital, workplace, or community rule.
- Make the protagonist speak for ordinary common sense after many comic humiliations.
- The final reversal should change the moral interpretation of "reasonable".

## Choosing A Style For A Short Drama

| User story | Primary engine | Secondary engine |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 豪门婚姻/婆媳/继承 | 《大红灯笼高高挂》 ritual ranking | 《满城尽带黄金甲》 luxury suffocation |
| 农村维权/亲属冲突 | 《秋菊打官司》 procedural persistence | 《黄土地》 land/fate imagery |
| 女性复仇/被压迫婚恋 | 《菊豆》 enclosed desire/prison | 《红高粱》 bodily rebellion |
| 校园/留守儿童/老师 | 《一个都不能少》 one objective | 《我的父亲母亲》 repeated care |
| 悬疑密室/反转喜剧 | 《满江红》 sealed ensemble | 《影》 doubles and deception |
| 都市权力/黑幕 | 《坚如磐石》 neon institution | 《英雄》 competing truths |
| 战争/谍战/牺牲 | 《悬崖之上》 cold secrecy | 《一秒钟》 image as evidence |
| 亲情失忆/旧爱重逢 | 《归来》 repeated waiting | 《活着》 family endurance |

## Minimum Extraction Rule

For each chosen film-period reference, extract at least:

- one color rule;
- one space rule;
- one repeated ritual or action;
- one symbolic object;
- one crowd/authority arrangement;
- one sound motif;
- one ending-image principle.
